The Method

How we teach analytical writing.

Most students struggle because they don't have a system. We break down the complex process of literary analysis into three visual, tangible steps.

01

Annotating the Text

Analysis starts with reading. We don't just tell students "this is a metaphor". We show them how to find it.

In our live demonstrations, we trace the patterns on screen, highlighting the exact words and devices the author uses. This trains the student's eye to spot these patterns themselves.

02

Structured Activities

Understanding isn't enough; they need to practice.

Our worksheets aren't just quizzes. They are scaffolded activities that guide students to link the Device (what the author used) to the Meaning (why they used it).

03

Constructing Analysis

Finally, we turn observations into arguments.

We use a sentence-building framework (T-V-O-D-E) that helps students construct sophisticated analytical paragraphs. They learn to weave evidence and explanation together seamlessly.
