Romeo and Juliet Analysis

The Dread Engine

The Prologue is a spoiler. Shakespeare kills suspense in the first 30 seconds to build something more devastating: Dread.

Most stories ask: "What will happen?"
Romeo & Juliet asks: "How will I watch it happen?"

Step 1 ¬∑ The Master Mechanic

Prophetic Witnessing

Shakespeare invented a pattern that makes tragedy feel inevitable. Once you see it, you can't unsee it.

1

The Prophecy

The Prologue announces the ending.

2

The Gap

Characters have hope. We have knowledge.

3

The Dread

We watch them pursue hope toward doom we already know.

"My grave is like to be my wedding bed."
‚Äî Juliet, Act 1 Scene 5

Juliet means: "If he's already married, I'll die unmarried."

We hear: "She's right‚Äîher wedding will lead to her grave."

Characters speak more truly than they know.
