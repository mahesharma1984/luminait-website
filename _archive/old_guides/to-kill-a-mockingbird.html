‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions

What is the main theme of To Kill a Mockingbird? +
The novel explores how systemic racism corrupts supposedly moral communities, revealed through a child's innocent observations. Scout's fresh perspective exposes the gap between American ideals of equality and the reality of institutional prejudice in 1930s Alabama.
What literary devices are used in To Kill a Mockingbird? +
Lee primarily uses dramatic irony between child and adult perspectives, symbolism (like Tom's disabled hand), and unfiltered social dialogue. These devices work together to create Scout's function as a moral witness who exposes corruption through innocent observation.
How do I write an essay about To Kill a Mockingbird? +
Focus on how Scout's innocent perspective reveals adult corruption and systemic racism. Use specific quotes showing her unfiltered observations of prejudice, then analyze how these moments expose broader social failures and moral hypocrisy.
What makes To Kill a Mockingbird a classic? +
The novel's enduring power lies in its innocent narrator who makes familiar prejudices newly visible and morally urgent. Scout's child perspective strips away adult rationalization, creating a timeless critique of how good people can enable systemic injustice.
