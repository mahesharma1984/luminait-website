The Giver

10-week analysis course for Lois Lowry's text.

In this 10-week course, students learn to analyze how Lowry uses euphemistic language and dramatic irony to critique totalitarian control. We move beyond plot summary to examine the 'Control Machine'—the specific mechanisms this society uses to disguise oppression as perfection.

Course Breakdown

Week 1

The Perfect Society

Chapters 1-2 • Focus: Euphemism
Identifying how language masks reality (e.g., 'Release')
Week 2

The Rules of Control

Chapters 3-5 • Focus: Symbolism
Analyzing the Pale Eyes and the Apple as symbols of difference
Week 3

The Ceremony

Chapters 6-9 • Focus: Juxtaposition
Contrasting the community's order with Jonas's inner uncertainty
Week 4

The Awakening

Chapters 10-12 • Focus: Sensory Imagery
Analyzing the introduction of color and memory
Week 5

The Burden of Truth

Chapters 13-16 • Focus: Dramatic Irony
Understanding what Jonas knows that his family does not
Week 6

The Climax

Chapters 17-19 • Focus: The 'Release' Scene
Analyzing the pivotal moment of realization (Video analysis)
Week 7

The Escape

Chapters 20-23 • Focus: Narrative pacing
Tracking the shift from passive acceptance to active rebellion
Week 8

Thesis Construction

Essay Planning • Focus: Argument Structure
Formulating a claim about Freedom vs. Security
Week 9

Evidence Integration

Essay Drafting • Focus: TEEL Structure
Embedding quotes seamlessly into analysis
Week 10

Complete Essay

Final Assessment • Focus: Polishing
Submitting a completed analytical essay

What Students Produce

Student analysis of the 'Release' scene

"Lowry uses the Father's casual tone during the twin's release to create a chilling contrast between his gentle actions and the brutality of infanticide. This euphemistic language reveals how the community has stripped death of its meaning to maintain compliant order."
