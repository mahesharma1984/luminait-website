Parent Curriculum Guide

Animal Farm

George Orwell · Years 9-10

Allegorical Power Corruption

Choose the course length to see the curriculum

1
Foundation

Understanding the Text

Building foundational understanding of the allegory

  • Introduction to Animal Farm and Russian Revolution context
  • Understanding the farm setting and character parallels
  • Meeting the animals and their symbolic roles
  • Identifying the allegorical structure
2
Foundation

How the Text Works

Discovering how Orwell constructs political satire

  • Exploring the central pattern: Power Corruption
  • Understanding how revolution becomes tyranny
  • Recognising recurring motifs (Seven Commandments, songs, slogans)
  • Analysing how allegory conveys political critique
3
Analysis

Analysing Key Techniques

Learning to identify and analyse Orwell's techniques

  • Technique 1: Allegory (animal characters as historical figures)
  • Technique 2: Satire (mocking totalitarianism)
  • Technique 3: Propaganda Language (Squealer's manipulation)
  • Collecting textual evidence for each technique
4
Analysis

Building the Argument

Developing a thesis and analytical argument

  • Exploring the guiding question: How does Orwell use allegory to reveal how revolutionary ideals are corrupted by those who seize power?
  • Forming a clear thesis statement
  • Connecting techniques to political themes
  • Planning the essay structure
5
Writing

Writing the Introduction

Crafting a compelling essay opening

  • Contextualising the novella effectively
  • Presenting the thesis statement
  • Previewing the analytical approach
  • Drafting and refining the introduction paragraph
6
Body Paragraphs

Body Paragraph 1

Deep analysis of Allegory

  • Crafting a clear topic sentence
  • Integrating textual evidence effectively
  • Analysing allegorical parallels
  • Linking analysis back to thesis
Allegory

Each animal character represents historical figures and social classes, allowing Orwell to critique Stalinism through accessible narrative.

7
Body Paragraphs

Body Paragraph 2

Deep analysis of Satire

  • Building on analytical skills from Week 6
  • Developing sophisticated quote integration
  • Exploring how satire exposes hypocrisy
  • Strengthening the analytical voice
Satire

Orwell's satirical tone mocks totalitarian rhetoric, exposing the gap between revolutionary promises and dictatorial reality.

8
Body Paragraphs

Body Paragraph 3

Deep analysis of Propaganda Language

  • Mastering the TEEL paragraph structure
  • Demonstrating sophisticated literary analysis
  • Connecting all three techniques thematically
  • Preparing for the essay conclusion
Propaganda Language

Squealer's manipulative language demonstrates how propaganda distorts truth and maintains power through deception.

9
Completion

Cohesion & Conclusion

Creating a unified, polished essay

  • Writing a powerful conclusion
  • Adding transitions between paragraphs
  • Reviewing and strengthening argument flow
  • Proofreading for expression and accuracy
10
Completion

Complete Essay

Final polish and submission

  • Final draft completion
  • Peer review and feedback
  • Self-assessment against criteria
  • Celebration of analytical achievement

What Your Child Will Learn

Through studying Animal Farm, students will develop skills in:

  • Identifying and analysing allegorical structures
  • Understanding satire and political commentary
  • Analysing propaganda and manipulative language
  • Crafting evidence-based analytical arguments
  • Writing structured literary essays
  • Connecting textual analysis to historical context
